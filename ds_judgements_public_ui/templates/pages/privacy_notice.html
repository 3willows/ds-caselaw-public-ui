Who we are

The National Archives is a non-ministerial government department, and the official archive and publisher for the UK Government, and for England and Wales.

We receive judgments into the archive from the Courts, for permanent preservation and publication through the Find Case Law service. The Public Records Act 1958 (PRA) provides the legal basis for our work with judgments.

This privacy notice is specifically for the Find Case Law service. For The National Archive’s main privacy notice please view it on the main website..

Publication of your personal data

Judgments contain information about people. This typically includes the names of parties and other people mentioned in the judgment, as well as the representatives and the names of the judges hearing the case. The information about people varies and can be very extensive.

Publication of judgments is part of the administration of justice. It is necessary for open justice and for individuals to understand their rights and obligations. Publication of judgments supports the rule of law and transparency of the courts. As such it is in the public interest.

There are reporting restrictions in law that we comply with. A court or tribunal may decide to redact personal data within a judgment, such as an individual’s name or address. The court may also hold its proceedings in private and place restrictions on access to court and tribunal files. Such decisions are judicial decisions and can only be takennwithin the context of legal proceedings. Individuals wishing to request anonymisation or withdrawal of a judgment from publication should seek legal advice.

The Court that decided the case is the Data Controller for the publication of judgments on the Find Case Law service. The National Archives is a Data Processor. The National Archives cannot and will not takedown a published judgment without explicit instruction from the court. Revisions to judgments already published must also come directly from the court. Contact a specific court or tribunal.

Contact the Judicial Data Protection Panel

Court judgments are largely exempt from the requirements of Data Protection Law; therefore, the Information Commissioner has no regulatory function in relation to an individual acting in a judicial capacity, or a court or tribunal acting in its judicial capacity.

Concerns about processing of personal data in judgments should be addressed to the Judicial Data Protection Panel at {% translate "judiciary.email" %} as detailed here: https://www.judiciary.uk/wp-content/uploads/2019/04/Judicial- data-processing-complaints-handling-policy-June-2021.pdf .
